The attention gap may be identified as the difference between the known interest rate and the measured interaction rate. Under the assumption that the audience interaction rate would be similar to the interaction rate for the small high-quality panel if all users were involved in the content, the attention gap would therefore be Represents the percentage of users who do not or do not exist. If the item of content is indefinite, all users who pay attention to the presentation of the content will choose to skip or stop the presentation and thus respond with 100% viewer engagement or interest It may be assumed that there will be a final 100% skip rate. Thus, any difference between the theoretical 100% skip rate and the skip rate that can be extrapolated from the skip rate for shorter duration content represents the attention gap, or the percentage of non-engaged or absent users. In one such implementation, similar or identical content of varying lengths such as 30 seconds, 1 minute, 2 minutes, etc. may be provided to the viewer and the skip rate is measured. A best-fit curve may be determined and extrapolated for similar content over an infinite period so that the attention gap can be calculated. This gap may then be applied to early measurements to estimate the percentage of users involved and interested in each presentation of content.
