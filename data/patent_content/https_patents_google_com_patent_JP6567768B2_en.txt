Background One of the characteristics of a mobile device or wearable device is the time that the device can be operated, i.e., how long the device can continue to be used depending on the stored energy (eg, battery) capacity of the device. Most mobile devices are designed to automatically search or poll for signals from various wireless communication networks (eg, cellular phones, Wi-Fi, 3G, etc.) and / or which devices are currently mobile devices Designed to search or poll other devices that are using various wireless communication technologies (eg Bluetooth (registered trademark), Wi-Fi Direct (registered trademark), etc.) The Mobile devices typically utilize such wireless communication networks or technologies when transmitting or receiving data. However, each different type of wireless technology uses a different amount of power. Typically, mobile devices are configured to transmit data using the fastest available data connection (ie, the data connection with the maximum available bandwidth).
