Abstract
The present disclosure describes apparatus and techniques for adaptive PLMN management for varying network conditions. In some aspects, as part of a registration process, a PLMN manager (144) of a user equipment (UE, 102) searches a data repository including PLMN information for PLMNs (104, 106) that are prevented from registration for a duration of time, and determines that the PLMNs are prevented due to signal related conditions (e.g., weak signals) associated with cells (110, 112) of the PLMNs. The PLMN manager (144) then determines that signal correlation conditions between the UE (102) and the cells (110, 112) have improved during the duration. In response to the improvement, the PLMN manager (144) unblocks the PLMN (104, 106) prior to expiration of the duration. By doing so, the UE (102) may re-attempt to register with the unblocked PLMN (104, 106) at least once before the duration expires, which may allow the UE to register faster and re-establish network services.
Description
Technical Field
The present application relates to an adaptive public land mobile network management apparatus and method for varying network conditions.
Background
The popularity of wireless communication technology has greatly increased the number of devices capable of accessing remote data or other services over a wireless network. For wide area networks, such as cellular networks, a core network operator typically provides telecommunication services to mobile stations throughout the network. One example of a network is a Public Land Mobile Network (PLMN) comprising a core network and base stations. The base stations collectively form a Radio Access Network (RAN) that is connected to the network. To communicate wirelessly with the network, the mobile station may establish a connection to the core network via a cell (e.g., base station, serving cell) of the PLMN.
During the ATTACH (atach) procedure to establish this connection, the network will query about the wireless communication capabilities supported by the mobile station. In order to attach successfully to a PLMN, the mobile station needs to respond to the network with its capabilities including supported wireless communication bands and other parameters. To increase the data rate, some mobile stations and networks implement Carrier Aggregation (CA) that allows the mobile station to use multiple channels to communicate data with the network. To enable carrier aggregation, the mobile station also communicates various combinations of CA parameters such as carrier, frequency band, and bandwidth to the network as part of its wireless communication capabilities.
However, as networks deploy more CA-supporting cells with multiple frequency bands, the number and size of CA parameter combinations supported by many mobile stations has grown significantly. With this increase in the number and size of CA parameter combinations, the capability messages of mobile stations transmitted to the network during the attach procedure also increase substantially in size. In case of poor network coverage or weak signal strength, transmission of capability messages comprising CA parameter combinations often takes longer to complete due to reduced network throughput. Due to the guard time implemented by many network operators, a connection with a mobile station may be dropped during the attach procedure when no full capability message is received from the mobile station within the guard time. Additionally, after a number of failed attach procedures, the mobile station will prevent the PLMN from proceeding with subsequent attach procedures for a certain length of time. As a result, mobile stations with poor network coverage often cannot complete an attach procedure with a PLMN or may be prevented from retrying the attach procedure for a length of time during which the mobile station's network service may be compromised or denied.
Disclosure of Invention
The present disclosure describes apparatus and techniques for adaptive PLMN management for varying network conditions. In some aspects, a user equipment performs a method that includes searching, as part of a registration process, a data repository including PLMN information for PLMNs that are prevented from registering for a predefined duration. The method then determines that the PLMN that is blocked from registration is blocked due to signal related conditions of the connection between the UE and the cell of the PLMN. The method further comprises determining that signal related conditions of a connection between the UE and a cell of the PLMN have improved during the predefined duration. In response to an improvement in the signal-related condition, the method unblocks PLMN registration. The method then transmits a request for registration with the unblocked PLMN to a cell of the PLMN to enable registration with the PLMN prior to expiration of the predefined duration.
In other aspects, a user equipment performs a method that includes transmitting a first request to a cell of a PLMN for registration with the PLMN. The method then detects a failure of the first request for registration with the PLMN during a phase of sending the UE capability message. The method comprises the following steps: determining that a signal-related characteristic for communicating with a cell of the PLMN fails to meet a threshold; and identifying a PLMN registration failure based on the signal correlation characteristic. The method then determines whether the signal related characteristics for communication with the cells of the PLMN remain unchanged, improve or degrade throughout the duration. In response to the determination of the signal correlation characteristic, the method uses fewer or more CA combinations in the UE capability message during a next request for registration with the PLMN. If the signal condition improves significantly, the UE may make another registration attempt before expiration of the duration.
In other aspects, a user equipment includes: a transceiver operatively coupled with one or more antennas; a hardware-based processor associated with the transceiver; and a computer readable storage medium storing instructions executable by the hardware-based processor of the UE. The instructions are executable to implement a PLMN manager configured to direct the user equipment to search a data repository including PLMN information for PLMNs that are prevented from being registered for a predefined duration as part of a registration process. The PLMN manager determines that the PLMN prevented from registering is prevented due to a signal related condition of a connection between the UE and a cell of the PLMN. The PLMN manager then determines, via the transceiver and during the duration, that signal related conditions of the connection between the UE and the cell of the PLMN have improved. In response to the improvement of the signal related condition, the PLMN manager unblocks PLMN registration. The PLMN manager then transmits, via the transceiver and to a base station of the PLMN, a request for registration with the unblocked PLMN to enable registration with the PLMN prior to expiration of the predefined duration.

Detailed Description
SUMMARY
The user equipment accesses a cellular network, such as a Public Land Mobile Network (PLMN), through a communication link or "connection" with a base station or serving cell of the network. As part of establishing or maintaining a connection, a User Equipment (UE) may register with the PLMN, such as through an attach procedure or Tracking Area Update (TAU). When the UE attempts to register with the PLMN (network), the network will query the UE for wireless communication capabilities. For successful registration, the UE may respond to the network with its wireless communication capabilities (e.g., UE capability messages) to enable communication between the UE and the network. Some UEs and PLMNs provide increased bandwidth through Carrier Aggregation (CA) that enables the UE to use multiple channels to communicate data with the network. To enable carrier aggregation with the network, these UEs also communicate various combinations of CA parameters such as carrier, band and bandwidth to the network as part of their UE capability messages.
However, as networks deploy more CA-supporting cells with multiple frequency bands, the number and size of CA parameter combinations supported by many UEs has grown significantly. With this increase in the number and size of CA parameter combinations, UE capability messages transmitted during registration with a PLMN have also increased substantially in size. In case of poor network coverage or weak signal strength, the transmission of UE capability messages comprising a combination of CA parameters often takes longer to complete due to reduced network throughput. Due to the guard time implemented by many network operators, the network may release a connection (e.g., a Radio Resource Control (RRC) connection) with the UE during registration if a full UE capability message is not received within the guard time. After a registration failure or a connection is released, the UE is forced to re-attempt registration through another attach procedure. According to conventional techniques for PLMN selection, the UE may only make five registration attempts before preventing a PLMN by adding the PLMN to the temporarily forbidden PLMN list for a certain duration (e.g., 12 minutes per T3402 timer). Thus, the UE may block the preferred PLMN due to poor previous network conditions, which may cause denial of service or impairment of service for a considerable duration. In some cases, the PLMN remains blocked throughout the duration (e.g., 12 minutes) regardless of whether the UE moves back to good network coverage or network conditions improve, which may further frustrate the user of the UE.
The present disclosure describes apparatus and techniques for adaptive PLMN management for varying network conditions. As described, aspects of adaptive PLMN management may adaptively unblock PLMNs when signaling conditions of a UE improve, such as when the UE moves to an area with better network coverage. In general, as a result of adaptive PLMN unblocking, the UE may register with the PLMN faster than conventional technology permissions that lack any precautions for unblocking. In some cases, the UE may register with the PLMN after moving out of the bad signaling region (e.g., bad network conditions) without waiting for the entire duration (e.g., 12 minutes) as specified by the various communication specifications for PLMNs on the forbidden list.
As an example, in some scenarios, a UE receives coverage from only one cell of a PLMN and the cell may support only Long Term Evolution (LTE) services and not legacy access technologies. When poor network conditions (e.g., poor signaling) cause the UE to block the PLMN, the UE will typically block the PLMN and prevent the UE from receiving any voice or circuit switched services (e.g., legacy services) over LTE services for a significant length of time (e.g., 12 minutes). By implementing aspects of adaptive PLMN management, once the network conditions of the UE improve, such as when the UE moves to an area with medium or good network coverage, the UE may attempt registration faster (e.g., less than the full 12 minutes). In general, for a global UE with legacy support, the UE may be able to attempt registration based on expiration of a periodic Better System Reselection (BSR) timer (e.g., 90-120 seconds). Thus, aspects of adaptive PLMN management enable faster registration with PLMNs, such as when signaling conditions improve.
The present disclosure also describes apparatus and techniques for adaptive UE capability management for marginal network conditions. In some aspects, the UE capability manager may modify the UE capability information to increase the probability of successful registration under marginal network conditions (e.g., weak signal regions) without affecting the UE capability under good signal conditions. As noted, in some scenarios, a UE may receive coverage from only one cell of a PLMN and the cell may support only Long Term Evolution (LTE) services and not legacy access technologies. In these scenarios, the UE may be prevented from receiving any voice or circuit switched services from the LTE service for a significant length of time in response to multiple failed registration attempts. By implementing aspects of adaptive UE capability management, the UE may alternatively be able to register with a reduced or minimal set of UE capabilities, which enables the UE to receive voice and data services over LTE services. Additionally, by acquiring LTE services (or other third generation (3G) services), the UE is precluded from scanning legacy services (e.g., second generation (2G) services) for fallback services, thereby enabling the UE to save time and power.
In various aspects, as part of the registration process, a PLMN manager of the user equipment searches a data repository including PLMN information for PLMNs that are prevented from being registered for a duration of time, and determines that the PLMNs are prevented from being registered due to signal related conditions (e.g., poor signal coverage or poor signal area) of a connection between the UE and a cell of the PLMN. The PLMN manager then determines that signal related conditions between the UE and the cells of the PLMN have improved during the duration. In response to the improvement, the PLMN manager unblocks the PLMN. By doing so, the UE may re-attempt to register with the unblocked PLMN at least once before the duration expires, which may allow the UE to register faster and re-establish network services.

Fig. 4 illustrates a diagram of an example network protocol stack 400 through which a UE may communicate with entities of a PLMN in accordance with one or more aspects. In general, the network protocol stack 400 (stack 400) characterizes the communication system of the example environment 100 in which the UE 102 communicates with entities (such as cells or core networks) of the PLMNs 104 and 106. With reference to the network protocol stack, the terms "upper layer" and "lower layer" are relative to each other, wherein each layer in the network protocol stack is an "upper layer" for the layers that are lower ("lower layers") in the protocol stack. For example, the upper layer may correspond to a non-access layer and/or the lower layer may correspond to a medium access control layer or a radio link control layer.
In this example, the network protocol stack 400 includes a user plane 402 and a control plane 404. The upper layers of the user plane 402 and the control plane 404 share a common lower layer in the network protocol stack 400. A wireless device, such as UE 102 or base stations 108-114, implements each layer as an entity for communicating with another device using a respective protocol defined for that layer. For example, the UE 102 uses an RRC entity to communicate with a peer RRC entity in the base station 108 using an appropriate RRC protocol or RRC connection.
The shared lower layer includes a physical layer 406 (PHY layer 406), a medium access control layer 408 (MAC layer 408), a radio link control layer 410 (RLC layer 410), and a packet data convergence protocol layer 412 (PDCP layer 412). In general, the physical layer 406 provides hardware specifications for devices that communicate with each other and the MAC layer 408 specifies how to transfer data between the devices. The RLC layer 410 may provide a data transfer service to a higher layer in the network protocol stack 400, and the PDCP layer 412 provides a data transfer service to a higher layer in the network protocol stack 400.
Above the PDCP layer 412, the network protocol stack 400 of the wireless network is divided into a user plane 402 and a control plane 404. The layers of the user plane 402 include an internet protocol layer 414 (IP layer 414), a transport layer 416, and an application layer 418 that uses the wireless connection 116 or 118 to transfer data. Although not shown, the user plane may also include an optional Service Data Adaptation Protocol (SDAP) layer for quality of service (QoS) flow implementation and management in the 5G NR network. Generally, the IP layer 414 specifies how to transfer data from the application layer 418 to the destination node. Transport layer 416 may use Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) or User Datagram Protocol (UDP) to verify that data packets intended for transfer to the destination node arrive at the destination node for data transfer by application layer 418. In some implementations, the user plane 402 may also include a data service layer (not shown) that provides data transfer services to transfer application data, such as IP packets including web browsing content, video content, image content, audio content, or social media content, among others.
The control plane 404 includes a radio resource control layer 420 (RRC layer 420) and a non-access layer 422 (NAS layer 422). The RRC layer 420 establishes and releases radio connections and radio bearers, broadcasts system information, or performs power control. For example, during registration with one of the PLMNs 104 or 106, the UE 102 may request an RRC connection by sending an RRC connection request message to one of the base stations 108 to 114 (e.g., eNB or cell) along with an establishment cause field (e.g., a "mobile originated signaling" value) for requesting attachment, detachment, or performance of a TAU. The UE 102 may then initiate registration with the PLMN 104 or PLMN 106 using the RRC connection, such as by transmitting an attach request message or TAU message to the network core via the NAS layer 422. As noted, poor cell coverage or weak signals for a connection may cause a registration attempt to fail, which may be detected as a registration failure due to release of an RRC connection by the network or due to an underlying cause (e.g., radio link failure), etc.
NAS layer 422 provides support for mobility management and packet data bearer context between UE 102 and entities or functions in the core network, such as MME 140 of EPC 124 or AMF 142 of 5gc 126. In UE 102, each layer of both user plane 402 and control plane 404 of network protocol stack 400 interacts with a corresponding peer layer or entity, core network entity or function, and/or remote service in the cell to support user application and control operations of UE 102 in RAN 120 or RAN 122.

Fig. 6 illustrates an example method 600 for PLMN registration in accordance with one or more aspects. In general, the method 600 may implement adaptive UE capability management or adaptive PLMN management to enable registration with PLMNs under varying (e.g., improved) or marginal (e.g., poor signaling) network conditions. In some aspects, the operations of the method 600 are implemented by or using the PLMN manager 144, the UE capability manager 146, the PLMN information 232, and/or the UE capability information 234 of the user equipment 102.
At 602, a UE initiates registration with a Public Land Mobile Network (PLMN). Registration with the PLMN may include an attach request or a Tracking Area Update (TAU) request. In some cases, the attach request or TAU request includes a UE capability message of a band supported by the UE, a Radio Access Technology (RAT), and/or a Carrier Aggregation (CA) band combination. Alternatively or additionally, registering may include establishing an RRC connection with a cell or base station of the PLMN.
As an example, consider fig. 7, which illustrates an example message sequence diagram for PLMN registration in accordance with one or more aspects. Here, it is assumed that the UE 102 is attempting to register with the base station 110 of the PLMN 104 to access network services. For registration, the UE 102 sends an RRC connection request message 702 to the base station 110 (eNB or cell) of the PLMN 104. The UE 102 then receives an RRC connection setup message 704 including radio resource configuration information (not shown) from the base station 110. With the radio resource configuration information provided by the base station 110, the UE establishes an RRC connection 706 and may send an RRC connection complete message 708 (e.g., if sent separate from the attach request or TAU request).
Referring to fig. 7, the ue 102 then sends a request for registration, such as an attach request message or TAU request message, using the active RRC connection 706 at 710. The attach request message or TAU request message sent to the base station also includes a ue_ CapabilityInformation message 712 describing the frequency bands supported by the UE, radio Access Technology (RAT), and/or carrier aggregation frequency band combinations. As noted, the UE CapabilityInformation message 712 may have a substantial size due to the number of carrier aggregation band combinations supported by the UE 102.
Returning to fig. 6, the ue detects a failure to register with the PLMN at 604, which may include a failure of an attach request or a failure of a TAU request. The registration failure may include release of an RRC connection, radio link failure, or failure due to an underlying cause. In some cases, the UE may determine that an amount of time between transmission of the UE capability message and release of the RRC connection is greater than a predefined value (e.g., three seconds). This may indicate that the UE cannot successfully transmit the entire UE capability message to the network within the guard time. In the context of the present example, when an RRC release message 716 is received from the base station 110 and the RRC connection is released by the UE 102 at 718, an attach request or TAU request failure is detected at 714.
At 606, the UE determines that the registration failure is due to a signal related condition of a connection with a cell of the PLMN. The UE may measure or monitor signal related characteristics of the connection with the cell of the PLMN. In some cases, the UE compares the signal correlation characteristics to a threshold (e.g., -110dBm, -115dBm, or-120 dBm) to determine a signaling state (e.g., bad signaling) or network condition (e.g., bad or marginal condition) associated with a cell of the PLMN. The signal correlation characteristics may include one or more of the following: received signal strength, received signal quality, reference signal received power, reference signal received quality, received signal strength indicator, carrier to interference ratio, signal to noise ratio, bit error rate, or packet error rate.
At 608, the UE implements adaptive UE capability management in response to the determination regarding the signal related conditions. In general, the UE may implement adaptive UE capability management in response to determining that the registration failure is due to or associated with poor signaling, marginal network conditions, or poor coverage provided by the cell. In some aspects, the UE predicts (e.g., through machine learning) or estimates a number of CA band combinations that can be successfully transmitted to the network, such as based on CA band combinations sent in a previous successful registration attempt.
The UE then reduces or removes the CA information (e.g., one or more combinations of CA frequency bands) from the UE capability message to provide a modified capability message that is smaller in size. In some cases, the CA information is reduced based on a prediction or estimate of the UE. In other cases, the decrease in CA information may be predetermined, such as by half for each iteration of retrying registration. By doing so, the UE may increase the chance of successful registration with the PLMN when re-attempting registration with the modified capability message. Continuing with the ongoing example, UE 102 implements adaptive UE capability management at 720 to reduce the amount of CA information in UE CapabilityInformation message 712, which reduces the size of the message.
At 610, the UE retries registration with the PLMN using the modified UE capability message. This may include transmitting another attach request or TAU request with a modified UE capability message including a reduced number of CA information or a subset of CA information. The UE may re-attempt registration multiple times up to a threshold of maximum number of attempts or failures (e.g., five attempts or failures). With each iteration of adaptive UE capability management, the UE may remove the additional CA information with each retry registration. Alternatively or additionally, the UE may remove all CA information from the registration request, such as at the last registration attempt (e.g., fifth attempt).
Referring to fig. 7, the ue 102 retries registration with the PLMN 104 for attachment or TAU at 722. To do so, the UE 102 may reestablish the RRC connection 706 (if needed) and send another attach request or TAU request. The attach request or TAU request to retry registration includes a modified UE CapabilityInformation message with a subset or less carrier aggregation band combination than the previous request. As noted, the UE 102 may repeat multiple iterations of adaptive UE capability management such that subsequent registration requests include fewer and fewer CA band combinations to iteratively improve the probability of successful registration.
Returning to fig. 6, the method 600 may proceed to 612 where registration is accepted by the PLMN or to 614 where registration is unsuccessful. At 612, successful registration may be the result of retrying registration with a modified UE capability message that includes reduced or removed CA information. In general, adaptive EU capability management may enable user equipment to complete registration using modified UE capability messages under marginal or poor signaling conditions (which typically prevent registration due to low connection throughput).
At 614, the UE determines that registration has failed due to the maximum number of attempts. In some aspects, PLMN registration is prevented by adding the PLMN to the temporary PLMN list in response to the maximum number of attempts. In the context of the present example, UE 102 determines at 724 to exceed a registration (attach/TAU) attempt count.
At 616, the UE implements adaptive PLMN management in response to the determination regarding network conditions and registration failure. As noted, PLMN registration may be inhibited for a duration after a number of failed registration attempts. Here, the UE may search for available cells or preferred PLMNs or monitor network conditions of PLMNs while being blocked. Continuing with the ongoing example, after the attempt count is exceeded and the PLMN is placed on the temporary forbidden PLMN list, UE 102 implements adaptive PLMN management at 726.
At 618, the UE unblocks the PLMN to reattempt registration in response to the improvement in network conditions. As described, the UE may adaptively unblock the PLMN when signaling conditions of the UE improve, such as when the UE moves to an area with improved network coverage. As a result, the UE can register with the PLMN faster than is permitted by conventional techniques. In some cases, the UE can register with the PLMN after moving out of the bad signaling region (e.g., bad network conditions) without waiting for the entire duration (e.g., 12 minutes) as specified by the various communication specifications for PLMNs on the forbidden list. Ending the present example, the UE may try to do so by re-attempting registration by returning to 722 until the attempt/failure count is exceeded again and the PLMN is re-blocked.
Fig. 8 illustrates an example method 800 for adaptive UE capability management for marginal network conditions. In general, the method 800 reduces the amount of Carrier Aggregation (CA) information in a UE capability message for registration under poor network conditions. In some aspects, the operations of the method 800 are implemented by or using the PLMN manager 144, the UE capability manager 146, the PLMN information 232, and/or the UE capability information 234 of the user equipment 102.
At 802, the UE transmits a UE capability message to the base station as part of a request for registration with a PLMN. The request for registration may include an attach request or a TAU request for the PLMN. In some cases, the attach request or TAU request includes a UE capability message of a band supported by the UE, a Radio Access Technology (RAT), and/or a Carrier Aggregation (CA) band combination.
At 804, the UE detects a failure to register with the PLMN. The failure of registration may include a failure of an attach request or a failure of a TAU request. The failure to register with the PLMN may include release of RRC connection, radio link failure, or failure due to lower layer reasons. In some cases, the UE may determine that an amount of time between transmission of the UE capability message and release of the RRC connection is greater than a predefined value (e.g., three seconds).
At 806, the UE determines that a signal-related condition for communicating with a base station of the PLMN fails to meet a predefined threshold. The UE may compare the signal correlation characteristics to a predefined threshold (e.g., -110dBm, -115dBm, or-120 dBm) to determine a signaling state (e.g., bad signaling) or network condition (e.g., bad or marginal condition) associated with the cells of the PLMN. From the determination operation of 806, the method 800 can proceed to operation 808 and/or operation 810, such as when a maximum number of registration attempts have not been made. Alternatively, the method 800 may proceed from operation 806 to operation 812, such as when the UE determines that the next registration attempt is the last registration attempt before the maximum number of registration attempts will be met.
At 808, the UE reduces the amount of CA information in the UE capability message. The UE may reduce the amount of CA information in response to a determination that the signal-related conditions of the connection with the base station are poor (e.g., weak signal coverage). In general, reducing the amount of CA information in the UE capability message includes removing many CA band combinations from the UE capability message. In some cases, the UE predicts (e.g., through machine learning) or estimates the number of CA band combinations that can be successfully transmitted to the network, such as based on CA band combinations sent in a previous successful registration attempt. These CA band combinations may then be removed to reduce the size of the UE capability message.
At 810, the UE retries registration with the PLMN using the UE capability message with reduced CA information. This may include transmitting another attach request or TAU request with the modified UE capability message including a reduced number of CA information or a subset of CA information at operation 802. If the retry registration is successful, the UE may then access services available through the PLMN. Alternatively or additionally, the UE may reattempt to register multiple times up to a threshold of a maximum number of attempts or failures (e.g., five attempts or failures). With each iteration of adaptive UE capability management, the UE may remove the additional CA information with each retry registration. Thus, method 800 may repeat operations 802 through 810 until a maximum number of registrations are attempted. During the last iteration, method 800 may proceed from operation 806 to operation 812 in response to the UE determining that the next registration attempt is the last registration attempt before the maximum number of registration attempts will be met.
At 812, in response to the multiple registration failures, the UE removes the CA information from the UE capability message. For example, as a final attempt to register with the PLMN, the UE may remove any remaining CA band combinations from the UE capability message to minimize the size of the message and further increase the likelihood of successful registration with the network. In such a case, the UE capability message may include the minimum UE capability including the frequency band and RAT supported by the UE.
At 814, the UE retries registration with the PLMN using the UE capability message from which the CA information was removed. This may include transmitting another attach request or TAU request with the modified UE capability message including the minimum CA information or not including CA information at operation 802. If the registration is successful, the UE may then access services available through the PLMN. Alternatively, if registration is unsuccessful, the UE may block the PLMN and implement adaptive PLMN management to enable fast reattachment when network conditions improve.

In general, the attach request or TAU request may include UE capability messages for frequency bands supported by the UE, radio Access Technology (RAT), and/or Carrier Aggregation (CA) band combinations.
Optionally, at 904, the UE detects a signal related condition of the cell. In some cases, the UE compares a signal-related characteristic (e.g., RSRP or RSRQ) to a predefined threshold (e.g., -110dBm, -115dBm, or-120 dBm) to determine a signaling state (e.g., bad signaling) or network condition (e.g., bad or marginal condition) associated with a cell of the PLMN.
At 906, the UE detects release of a Radio Resource Control (RRC) connection with the base station. The release of the RRC connection may be detected after transmitting the UE capability information to the cell of the PLMN. Alternatively or additionally, the lower layer failure may cause a failure of registration or loss of RRC connection with a cell of the PLMN.
At 908, the UE determines that a duration between transmission of the UE capability information and release of the RRC connection meets a threshold. For example, the UE may determine that the amount of time between transmission of the UE capability message and release of the RRC connection is greater than three seconds (or another guard time). This may indicate that the UE cannot successfully transmit the entire UE capability message to the network within the guard time.
From the RRC release operation of 908, the method 900 can proceed to operation 910 and/or operation 912, such as when a maximum number of registration attempts have not been made. Alternatively, the method 900 may proceed from operation 908 to operation 914, such as when the UE determines that the next registration attempt will meet the threshold for the maximum number of registration attempts.
At 910, the UE determines a number of CA combinations to be removed from the UE capability message. In some cases, the UE determines or predicts (e.g., by machine learning) the number of CA band combinations that can be successfully transmitted to the network, such as based on the number or size (e.g., number of bytes) of CA band combinations sent in a previous successful registration attempt. Alternatively, the UE may remove a predetermined portion, ratio, or fraction of the CA combination from the UE capability message with each iteration of operation 910.
At 912, the UE removes the determined number of CA combinations from the UE capability information. The method 900 may then return to operation 902, where the UE retries registration with the PLMN using UE capability information with fewer CA combinations. If the retry registration is successful, the UE may access services available through the PLMN. Alternatively, method 900 may perform additional iterations of operations 902 through 912 until a maximum number of registrations are attempted.
At 914, the ue determines that the next registration attempt will meet a threshold for the maximum number of registration attempts. In other words, the UE determines that the next re-attempt registration will be the last attempt before the PLMN is blocked for a certain duration.
At 916, the UE removes the remaining CA combination from the UE capability message. For example, as a final attempt to register with the PLMN, the UE may remove any remaining CA band combinations from the UE capability message to minimize the size of the message and further increase the likelihood of successful registration with the network. The UE then reattempts registration with the PLMN using the UE capability message from which the CA information was removed. If the last registration attempt is successful, the UE may then access the services available through the PLMN. Alternatively, if registration is unsuccessful, the UE may implement adaptive PLMN management once the PLMN is blocked.
Fig. 10 illustrates an example method 1000 of adaptive PLMN management for varying network conditions. In general, when the signal-related conditions improve, the method 1000 unblocks PLMNs identified as blocked due to the signal-related conditions. In some aspects, the operations of the method 1000 are implemented by or using the PLMN manager 144, the UE capability manager 146, the PLMN information 232, and/or the UE capability information 234 of the user equipment 102.
At 1002, a UE transmits a request to a cell for registration with a PLMN. The request for registration may include an attach request or a TAU request for the PLMN.
At 1004, the UE detects a failure to register with the PLMN. The failure may include a failure of an attach request or a failure of a TAU request. In some cases, the registration failure includes release of an RRC connection, a radio link failure, or a failure due to an underlying cause.
At 1006, the UE determines that the request for registration meets a threshold for a maximum number of registration attempts. In other words, the UE determines that a maximum number of registration attempts have been made before the PLMN is blocked for a certain duration.
At 1008, the UE prevents PLMN registration for a predefined duration. To block PLMNs, the UE may add the PLMNs to a temporary forbidden PLMN list configured to block PLMNs for a predefined duration (e.g., 12 minutes per T3402 timer).
At 1010, the UE determines that a signal-related characteristic for communicating with a cell of the PLMN fails to meet a predefined threshold. The UE may compare the signal correlation characteristics to a predefined threshold (e.g., -110dBm, -115dBm, or-120 dBm) to determine a signaling state (e.g., bad signaling) or network condition (e.g., bad or marginal condition) associated with the cells of the PLMN.
At 1012, the UE identifies the blocked PLMN as blocked based on the signal related characteristics. In some cases, the blocked PLMNs are marked, tagged, or identified in the PLMN database as blocked based on signal related conditions (such as a difference signal). Alternatively or additionally, this may enable the UE to track or monitor PLMNs on the temporarily forbidden PLMN list as candidate PLMNs for attachment when network conditions change or improve.
At 1014, the UE detects an improvement in signal-related characteristics of the blocked PLMN. The UE may monitor or measure signal related characteristics while the PLMN is blocked for a duration. Alternatively or additionally, the UE may periodically check network conditions to determine if signal correlation characteristics have improved. For example, a Better System Reselection (BSR) timer or other UE-specific event may trigger a cell scan in which the UE may detect an improvement or difference in the respective signal correlation characteristics of one or more cells.
At 1016, the UE unblocks the PLMN prior to expiration of the predefined duration. Typically, the UE unblocks the PLMN to enable another attempt when registering with the PLMN. In some cases, the UE unblocks the PLMN when the UE moves to an area with medium or good network coverage. In some aspects, detection of improved network conditions or unblocking may occur in response to a UE-specific event occurring during a duration that a PLMN will typically be blocked. Thus, adaptive PLMN unblocking may enable registration to be retried faster and/or with a greater frequency. For example, in the case of a 120 second BSR timer, the UE may check for signal conditions and/or retry registration (with 5 attempts) every two minutes, or six times within 12 minutes of the length of a typical temporary PLMN forbidden list timer.

In general, the method 1100 unblocks a blocked PLMN that is indicated by a flag due to poor network conditions when the PLMN meets network conditions (e.g., signal strength improvement). In some aspects, the operations of the method 1100 are implemented by or using the PLMN manager 144, the UE capability manager 146, the PLMN information 232, and/or the UE capability information 234 of the user equipment 102.
At 1102, the UE detects an attach/TAU failure with the PLMN. The attachment may be an initial attachment or the TAU may be a periodic TAU. At 1104, the UE determines whether the attachment failure is the largest or last registration failure that results in a block to the PLMN. At 1106, if the attachment failure is not a maximum registration failure (false), the UE takes no further action with respect to the PLMN that prevented the attachment failure from being detected. The UE may reattempt registration with the PLMN by transmitting another attach request or another tracking area update request.
At 1108, if the attach failure is a maximum registration failure (true), the UE blocks the PLMN by adding the PLMN to the temporary PLMN list. The PLMN may remain on the temporary PLMN list until the T3402 timer expires, at which point the PLMN may be automatically removed from the PLMN list.
At 1110, the UE determines whether network conditions between the UE and a base station of the PLMN are poor (e.g., poor signal coverage). The UE may compare the signal correlation characteristics to a predefined threshold (e.g., 110dBm, -115dBm, or-120 dBm) to determine a signaling state (e.g., bad signaling) or network condition (e.g., bad or marginal condition) associated with the cells of the PLMN. Alternatively or additionally, the UE may determine that the signaling condition is poor in response to release of the RRC connection, radio link failure, or registration failure due to an underlying cause. At 1112, if the network conditions are not bad (false), the UE does not take further action with respect to marking or tagging the blocked PLMN with a flag on the temporary PLMN forbidden list. In some cases, the UE waits until the timer of the PLMN forbidden list expires before retrying registration with the PLMN.
At 1114, the UE marks PLMNs on the temporary forbidden list as blocked due to poor network conditions. By doing so, the UE may track or monitor PLMNs while on the temporary PLMN forbidden list. From fig. 11A, method 1100 proceeds to fig. 11B, where the operations of method 1100 continue as shown at 1116.
At 1118, the UE initiates a new registration procedure. The registration procedure may be initiated in response to user selection, a Better System Reselection (BSR) procedure, a UE-specific procedure, etc. At 1120, the UE performs a PLMN search, such as to obtain a preferred or previously associated PLMN, through an acquisition database of locally stored PLMN information. In some cases, PLMN searches are performed in response to initiation of a new registration procedure or a better cell reselection procedure.
At 1122, the UE reviews the search results of the PLMN search for searched cells that may be associated with PLMNs that are blocked due to poor network conditions. At 1124, if the search cell is not serving a blocked PLMN (false), the UE takes no further action with respect to those cells. At 1126, if the network condition (e.g., signal strength) of the searched cell of the blocked PLMN meets a threshold for reattempting attachment on the blocked PLMN, the UE unblocks PLMN registration before removing from the temporary PLMN forbidden list before expiration of time. In other words, the UE will temporarily unblock the PLMN from the forbidden PLMN list and will reinitiate the attach or TAU registration procedure. At 1126, if the searched cell does not meet the threshold for re-attempting registration, method 1100 takes no further action with respect to the blocked PLMN. Alternatively, if no PLMN is found during the search, the UE may perform a full band PLMN search.
At 1132, the UE successfully registers with the PLMN. The UE may then access the network service or resource and the T3402 timer of the temporary PLMN prohibit list will be stopped. Alternatively, at 1134, if the registration is unsuccessful, the UE returns the PLMN to the temporary PLMN list. In some cases, the timer of the temporary PLMN prohibit list is not reset and the UE may unblock the PLMN multiple times before expiration of the list timer (e.g., returning to operation 1118).
