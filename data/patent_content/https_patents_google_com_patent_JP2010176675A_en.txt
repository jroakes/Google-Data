The contents that make up the conversion vary from case to case and can be determined in various ways. For example, a conversion may occur if a user clicks on an advertisement called an advertiser's web page and completes a purchase there before leaving the web page. Instead, a conversion may be defined as a purchase on the advertiser's web page within a predetermined time (eg, 7 days) after the user sees the advertisement. In yet another alternative, the conversion spends at least a predetermined amount of time on the website or web page, for example downloading a white paper, going to at least a predetermined depth of the website, displaying at least a certain number of web pages May be defined to the advertiser as a measurable / observable user action, such as registering on a website. The user actions that make up the conversion are not limited to this, but in many cases, if the user actions do not indicate completion of the purchase, they may indicate clues for sale. In fact, there are many other definitions of what constitutes a conversion. The ratio of the number of conversions to the number of ad impressions (that is, the number of times an ad is displayed) is generally called the conversion rate. If conversions are defined as occurring within a given time after the ad is served, then one possible definition of conversion rate may only consider ads that have been served more than a given time in the past There is sex.
